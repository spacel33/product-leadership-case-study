# Roadmap

## Overview
The roadmap is structured into four phases, each designed to reduce risk, improve predictability, and build toward long‑term reliability. Each phase delivers measurable improvements while enabling safe iteration.

---

## Phase 1 — Stabilization (Weeks 1–4)
Focus: Fix the most critical reliability issues.
- Deterministic retry logic  
- Worker concurrency patch  
- Queue depth monitoring  
- Hotfixes for peak‑load failures  

---

## Phase 2 — Predictability (Weeks 5–8)
Focus: Prevent cascading failures and load amplification.
- Traffic shaping  
- Rate limiting  
- Carrier routing optimization  
- Load‑aware retry behavior  

---

## Phase 3 — Observability (Weeks 9–12)
Focus: Enable proactive detection and faster mitigation.
- Latency dashboards  
- SLA anomaly alerts  
- Per‑customer delivery traces  
- Carrier‑specific performance metrics  

---

## Phase 4 — Hardening & Scale (Weeks 13–16)
Focus: Validate reliability under real‑world conditions.
- Load testing  
- Failover simulation  
- SLA validation  
- Incident response playbooks  

---

## Why This Roadmap Works
It balances:
- Immediate customer impact  
- Engineering feasibility  
- Risk reduction  
- Long‑term architectural health  

Each phase delivers value independently while building toward a more resilient system.
